Name of Flower:

Daisy

Scientific Name:

Bellis perennis

Fertilizers Used:

All-purpose fertilizer (10-10-10; equal amount of Nitrogen, Phosphorus and Potassium), fertilizer with high phosphorous.

Seasons:

Spring and autumn

Nurseries:

OM Nursery, Rajhans Nursery, Manakamana Nursery, Adhar Nursery

Summary:

Daisy are simple yet sophisticated and are some of the most beautiful flowers in the floral world. Daisy convey cheer and exuberance in spades. Not surprisingly, Daisy are popular both for gifting and growing in gardens. It was called Daisy because Daisy open at dawn as the day just starts to begin. Daisy symbolizes innocence and purity. It can also symbolize new beginnings. A Daisy is a perennial whose evergreen leaves form a basal tuft or a rosette. Daisy flower plant has a prostrate fashion or a growing habit of spreading. Daisy can be propagated by division in spring or through sowing seeds in spring or late autumn. English Daisy is a serious weed in the northwest United States. The Daisy flowers open at dawn and are visited by many small insects. Daisy are used by children to make Daisy chains. The Daisy's leaves are edible and can be used in salads. Daisy flower is composed of white petals and a yellow center, although the flower can sometimes have a pink or rose color. A Daisy is made up of two types of flowers - disk florets and petal-like white ray florets. The disk florets are at the center and the ray florets are at the periphery but they are arranged to give the impression of being a single flower. This arrangement on Daisy is a type of inflorescence known as a capitulum. The stems of Daisy are smooth and leafless and support a single flower. Daisy plants have 3 - 4 inch flower stalks. The Daisy leaf texture varies and may be smooth or hairy, narrow at the base and slightly lobed. The Daisy flower stalks are generally longer than the leaves. Daisy can be grown very easily. Daisy are hard perennials. Daisy are commonly grown from seeds. Daisy can be directly seeded into the flowerbed. Select a sunny location where they can grow undisturbed for years. Before planting, add generous amounts of manure and compost so that the soil becomes rich and well drained. If Daisy are planted in an area which is too hot, they should be shaded and watered regularly. Set the plants about 9 to 12 inches apart. Water the plants regularly during dry periods. Add a general purpose fertilizer in the early growth stage, and once a month afterwards. Generally Daisy are not bothered by insects and disease. If bothered by insects and disease, treat Daisy with an insecticidal soap or a fungicide. Just before blooming, use a fertilizer high in phosphorous as it helps to promote big, bright blooms. In cold weather regions, provide a thick layer of mulch for winter protection.
